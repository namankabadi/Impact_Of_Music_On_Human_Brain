System Architecture and Working:

The above figure shows the System architecture where training a machine learning model to analyze EEG data and study music's health effects. EEG data is collected while people listen to music, then cleaned and analyzed to extract brain activity patterns. The most informative features are chosen to train a model (like a ResNet-152) on identifying patterns linked to specific music genres. Splitting the data allows training and testing the model's ability to categorize music based on brainwaves. A well-trained model can then analyze new EEG data, potentially revealing how music genres influence brain activity and potentially our health.
